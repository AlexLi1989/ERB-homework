Rome

Ancient Grandeur & Timeless Beauty

Explore Rome

Welcome to Rome

Capital of Italy

Rome is the capital city and most populated comune of Italy. It is also the administrative centre of the Lazio region and of the Metropolitan City of Rome. Rome is the third most populous city in the European Union by population within city limits. Vatican City is an independent country inside the city boundaries of Rome, the only existing example of a country within a city.

Where Is Rome

All roads lead to Rome

Rome has a metropolitan area of 1,285 square kilometers (580 square miles). It is located along the southwestern coast of Italy, approximately 27 kilometers (17 miles) inland from the Tyrrhenian Sea. The ancient city was formed around the Tiber River and the seven significant hills of the region. The seven hills of Rome are the Palatine, Aventine, Capitoline, Caelian, Esquiline, Quirinal, and Viminal hills.

Best Time to Visit

Spring and Autumn

Spring: April to May

Spring is one of the best times to visit Rome, especially if you’re a fan of walking tours, photography, and outdoor activities. The temperatures are perfect for exploring Rome’s ancient sites without the scorching summer heat. Plus, the crowds are still manageable, making it easier to take in the sights.

Autumn: September to October

Autumn is another fantastic time to visit Rome, especially if you're a foodie or culture enthusiast. The weather is still warm early on, but it becomes much more comfortable as the months progress. Plus, autumn in Rome is harvest season, so you’ll find markets filled with fresh produce!
