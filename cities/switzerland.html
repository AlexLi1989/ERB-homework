Explore Bern

Welcome to Bern

Capital of Switzerland

Bern is the de facto capital of Switzerland, referred to as the "federal city". With a population of about 146,000 (as of 2024), Bern is the fifth-most populous city in Switzerland. Bern is also the capital of the canton of Bern, the second-most populous of Switzerland's cantons. In addition to being the Swiss federal capital, Bern is the headquarters of the international postal, telegraph, railway, and copyright unions.

Where Is Bern

A Medieval Fairy Tale On the Aare

Bern is the capital city of Switzerland, located in the west-central part of the country within the Canton of Bern. It sits on the Swiss Plateau, nestled along a narrow, bend-filled loop of the Aare River, roughly 20 km north of the Bernese Alps. Situated in the heart of the Canton of Bern, the city is a key hub in west-central Switzerland.

Best Time to Visit

Summer and Two Shoulder Seasons

Summer: June to August

Summer is the busiest season of Bern. This is the time when all hiking trails, hotels, museums, art galleries and outdoor activities are fully open and operational. Swimming is a favourite local past time and the riverfront echoes with the cries of laughter and joy. This this is the warmest and the rainiest season of the year. But the rains won’t affect your sightseeing plans.

Shoulder Season

Bern has two shoulder seasons and they’re the perfect time to visit if you want to enjoy your holidays without the crowd. Spring (March to May) and Autumn (September to November) are the in-between seasons which will allow you to have hassle-free holidays. March is still cold, but the rest of the spring ranges between cool to pleasant. Autumn begins on a warm note but temperature dips quickly as winter approaches. While September and October are mostly pleasant, November gets really cold as temperatures slide to single digit.

Wonders of Bern

A Magical One-day Trip

8:00 AM – Breakfast: Einstein Café (historic café linked to Einstein’s residence)
9:30 AM – Zytglogge Clock Tower (iconic medieval clock with hourly show)
11:00 AM – Bundesplatz & Federal Palace (Swiss Parliament building)
12:30 PM – Lunch: Kornhauskeller (grand vaulted cellar restaurant)
2:00 PM – Bear Park (BärenPark) (Bern’s symbolic animal in riverside park)
3:30 PM – Rose Garden (Rosengarten) (panoramic city views)
5:00 PM – Dinner: Restaurant Della Casa (classic Swiss cuisine)
6:30 PM – Bern Cathedral (Berner Münster) (tower climb for sunset views)
8:00 PM – Old Town Arcades (Kramgasse) (UNESCO heritage streets, open late)
