Welcome to Madrid

Capital of Spain

Madrid is the capital and most populous city of Spain. It had a population of over 3.4 million in the city proper in 2025. Madrid is the second-largest city in the European Union (EU), after Berlin and its metropolitan area is the second-largest in the EU, after Paris. As the capital city of both Spain and the surrounding autonomous community of Madrid, it is the political, economic and cultural centre of the country.

Where Is Madrid

Madrid, The Heart of Spain

Madrid lies in the centre of the Iberian peninsula on the southern Meseta Central, 60 km south of the Guadarrama mountain range and straddling the Jarama and Manzanares river sub-drainage basins, in the wider Tagus River catchment area. With an average altitude of 650 m (2,130 ft), Madrid is the second highest capital of Europe, after Andorra la Vella.

Best Time to Visit

Spring and Autumn

Spring: April to June

Things start to heat up in the Spanish capital come mid-March, and the temperature jumps considerably to an average daily high of 16°C – climbing to 23°C in May. Summer (between June and August) can be overwhelmingly hot, especially when sightseeing is on the agenda, so late spring and early summer are the best time to visit Madrid to enjoy all the city has to offer outdoors.

Autumn: September to October

As autumn approaches, the city starts to wrap up and the parks are covered in reddish leaves as they tumble from towering trees. However, this doesn’t happen until October, and temperatures can still reach the low 20s well into this month. The Americanised traditions of Halloween and the Mexican Día de Muertos (Day of the Dead) come together for an exciting programme of events, from the family-friendly to the late-night. Events happen across the city between the end of October and the beginning of November, resulting in some spooky cultural excursions.
