Explore Paris

Welcome to Paris

Capital of France

Paris, city and capital of France, situated in the north-central part of the country. For centuries Paris has been one of the world’s most important and attractive cities. It is appreciated for the opportunities it offers for business and commerce, for study, for culture, and for entertainment; its gastronomy, haute couture, painting, literature, and intellectual community especially enjoy an enviable reputation.

Where Is Paris

The City That Lives in The Heart

Paris is located in the north-central part of the country along the Seine River in the Île-de-France region. It lies about 233 miles (375 km) upstream from the river's mouth on the English Channel. The city is bisected by the Seine River from east to west. Few people know that there’s a second river in Paris, the Bièvre River, which flows through Paris underground.

Best Time to Visit

Spring and Early-Autumn

Spring: April to June

April marks the start of spring with blooming parks and milder temperatures. You can explore green spaces and see iconic landmarks like the under clear skies. Early-Autumn: September and October

September can be considered the best time to visit Paris without the peak-season pressure. With the comfortable weather, manageable crowds, and balanced energy, going around the city would be a great idea, especially for first-timers.
